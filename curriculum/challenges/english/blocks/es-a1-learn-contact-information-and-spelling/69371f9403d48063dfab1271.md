# --explanation--

`El tuyo` is a possessive pronoun in Spanish used to refer to something that belongs to the person being addressed (`tú`) and is masculine (for example, `número` or `libro`).

It is used when the noun has already been mentioned or is clear from the context, so it's not necessary to repeat it. For example:

- `Mi número es +57 313 445 9280. ¿Y el tuyo?` – My number is +57 313 445 9280. And yours?

- `Este es mi libro. El tuyo está aquí.` – This is my book. Yours is here.

In both cases, `el tuyo` replaces the noun and refers to something that belongs to the listener.
